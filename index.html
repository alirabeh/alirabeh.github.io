Biography

I am a machine learning engineer at Applied Materials working on generative design. I currently work on using machine learning to generate new designs for applications in the seminconductor industry. I have extensive experience in scientific machine learning, computational fluid dynamics, and scientific computing.

I completed my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at Iowa State University in 2023, advised by Dr. Baskar Ganapathysubramanian . My Ph.D. thesis focused on developing energy-stable solver for three-phase Cahn-Hilliard Navier-Stokes equations using an in-house finite-element framework. I also worked on simulating high density ratio two-phase flows in micro-gravity using a modified residual-based variational multiscale stabilization.

After my Ph.D., I worked as a postdoctoral research associate at the Translational AI Center at Iowa State University, advised by Dr. Baskar Ganapathysubramanian . My postdoctoral research focused on extedning state-of-the-art neural operators for modeling fluid flow around complex geometries. I also led the creation of an extensive benchmark dataset (FlowBench) to help test and advance scientific machine learning (SciML) models.

I am passionate about bridging scientific computing and deep learning, accelerating complex engineering applications, and developing methods and tools that serve the research community.

Research Projects

FlowBench Dataset

  • Led 6-member team to generate 8 TB data of flow around complex geometry using 100K compute hours.
  • Trained 11 neural operators and vision transformers foundation models using PyTorch Lightning.
  • Achieved 80% accuracy in flow prediction with 1000x speedup over CFD solvers.

Derivative-Informed Surrogates for 3D Flow

  • Simulated 3D driven-cavity flow around complex geometries using Shifted Boundary Method.
  • Developed Geometric-DeepONet with derivative/physics-informed loss.
  • Achieved 94% velocity and 82% gradient accuracy.

Time-Dependent DeepONet

  • Developed a time-dependent Geometric DeepONet model for flow prediction over varying geometries.
  • Forecasted flow over complex geometries with 95% accuracy for long time rollout.
  • Achieved 1000× speedup over CFD (0.01s inference time).

Three-Phase Flow

  • Developed a three-phase flow solver using the Cahn-Hilliard Navier-Stokes equations.
  • Implemented a degenerate mobility formulation to capture fine-scale dynamics.
  • Simulated complex flow of three-phase floating and rising bubbles in stratified fluids using AMR.

Two-Phase Flow

  • Developed a two-phase flow solver using the Cahn-Hilliard Navier-Stokes equations.
  • Simulated two-phase flows with up to 120K density ratio in 3D using adaptive mesh refinement.
